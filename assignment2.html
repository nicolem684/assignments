Attractions on Barton Springs Road

Austin, Texas

Barton Springs Road is a popular street just south of the Colorado River in Austin, Texas. Many popular restaurants and attractions are located off the road and the area experiences many visitors every day. Festivals and events such as Austin City Limits music festival and the Polar Bear Plunge. This website gives a brief tour of must-see things on Barton Springs Road, including Zilker Park, Barton Springs, and the UMLAUF Sculpture Garden.


Zilker Metro Park

The first must-see is Zilker Metro Park, Austin's largest public space and city park at over 350 acres. There are sand volleyball courts, picnic tables, food trucks, a disc golf course shady trees, and much more. Austin City Limits, ABC Kite Fest, and the Trail of Lights annual festivals are hosted here. The park is open almost year round, 5am to 10pm. It opened in 1917 and was named after Andrew Zilker who donated the land.


Barton Springs Pool

Barton Springs Pool is next on the list, just across the street from Zilker Park. This natural springs is filled by water from Edwards Aquifer at a cool 68 degrees. The Austin Blind Salamander is endemic to Barton Springs and Barton Creek, adding to the fragile and diverse ecosystem of the springs. Many rules are in place about what visitors can bring into the park, such as limits on spray sunscreen and glass containers. Barton Springs Pool hosts the Polar Bear Plunge every New Year's Day that is highly attended, with participants earning free t-shirts. The pool has an admissions fee that pays for the upkeep of the pool and lifeguard salaries, but there is a free swimming area right next to the maintained pool.


UMLAUF Sculpture Garden

UMLAUF Sculpture Garden is an outdoor museum filled with sculptures crafted by Charles Umlauf, a local sculptor and art professor who lived from 1911 to 1944. He and his wife owned a house overlooking Barton Springs Road and built a studio on the land as well. In 1985 they donated the land to the City of Austin for the public to enjoy. The City aquired more land adjacent to the lot, and eventually built the Sculpture Garden museum to honor Umlauf's work and life. The garden is open to the public, with discounts for certain community members. The museum hosts events, such as UMLAUF After Dark, for people to attend and get to experience the museum in a new way. Many weddings and parties are hosted in the event space every month as well.
